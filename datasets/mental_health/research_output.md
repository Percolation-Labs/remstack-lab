**1. Introduction: Current Challenges in CBT and the Need for Adaptive Protocols**

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is a widely recognized and effective treatment for a range of mental health conditions including depression, anxiety, and PTSD. However, traditional CBT delivery often relies on standardized protocols, which may not adequately address the unique needs and responses of individual patients. Even current digital CBT (dCBT) platforms often exhibit limited adaptability, restricting the therapist's ability to tailor interventions in real-time based on patient progress and physiological signals. This rigidity can hinder optimal treatment outcomes and increase patient dropout rates. Our research addresses this challenge by presenting a framework for dynamic CBT protocol optimization using a multi-modal data analysis approach. This system aims to identify optimal intervention sequences and individualize therapeutic strategies, ultimately enhancing treatment efficacy and accessibility. This architecture focuses on advanced optimization guidelines to facilitate immediate commercial development using currently validated technologies with a 5-10 year timeframe.
